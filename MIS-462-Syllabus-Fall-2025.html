Competence Statements/Course Learning Outcomes ‚ñº

Competence Statement

Knows the basic principles of distributed computing, the process of systems development and structured methodologies to accomplish these tasks, and understands the system life cycle. Can perform the functions required to accomplish these tasks at least at an introductory level.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Describe the concept of Information and distributed system.
  • Understand the issues unique to a distributed/service oriented architecture.
  • Discuss trends in distributed systems architecture.
  • Explain and implement concepts of Web Services, Service Oriented Architecture.
  • Explain concepts of cloud computing.
  • Explain and implement principles of managing change issues while working on Modernization (Web Services, Service Oriented Architecture & cloud computing) projects.

Learning Methods

This course uses a variety of assignments in order to measure student outcomes:

Course Policies ‚ñº

Student Expectations

Preparation and Student Effort

For a typical university course, you are expected to spend 3 to 4 hours per credit per week in your studies, preparation activities, and assessments. In a 4-credit course, you therefore are expected to spend 12 to 16 hours per week completing readings, assignments, discussions, and research for this course.

Late Assignments

Assignments are expected on the date due. Exceptions can only be made by prior arrangement with the instructor. The instructor is under no obligation to award any points at all for assignments submitted after the due date.

Attendance Policy

Federal Financial Aid regulations require that the University track the ongoing attendance of students. Students receiving financial aid are expected to maintain ongoing progress in activities within the course. Due to the participatory nature of this course, you may not earn competence if you miss more than two sessions.

Academic Integrity

Plagiarism Policy

Plagiarism is defined as the act of claiming another person's work as one's own. If a student is found to have plagiarized work, even if accidentally, the student will be provided a one-time opportunity to redo the assignment. Points will be reduced on the revised submission by up to ¬Ω (50%) of the total earned.

Important: Future work that is found to be plagiarized will be given a grade of zero (0). If plagiarism occurs repeatedly, course failure will result.

Students are expected to respect intellectual property laws, including copyright law. No parts of this site, your textbook, other publisher materials, or contribution from other class members can be used or distributed outside of this class without the permission of the author(s).

University Policies

Non-Attendance and Reporting Policy

The university needs to establish that students have attended at least one day of class for Federal Title IV financial aid regulations. A student is "in attendance" if they meet the following conditions before the end of the second week:

  • Classroom Courses: Student is present in the classroom
  • Web-Enhanced Courses: Student is present in classroom or submits academically relevant assignment
  • Online Courses: Student submits at least one academically relevant assignment
